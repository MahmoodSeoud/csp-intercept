A single-page INTEGRITY AUDIT REPORT for satellite operators, titled
"SatGuard — Upload-Path Integrity Audit", for the cubesat
"DISCO-2 — Aarhus University Student CubeSat".

Audience: aerospace flight-software engineers and satellite operators who
distrust anything that looks like marketing or AI-generated slop. The document
must read as a serious, INDEPENDENT TECHNICAL AUDIT — closer to a penetration-test
report or an instrument calibration certificate than a SaaS landing page. Its
credibility comes from restraint and typographic quality, not decoration.

Layout, top to bottom:
- a small MONOSPACE eyebrow/wordmark: "SATGUARD · UPLOAD-PATH INTEGRITY AUDIT"
- a large SERIF headline with the target name
- a thin monospace meta line: document id, date, one-line method
- a restrained VERDICT block: "VERDICT — VULNERABLE: 3 of 4 upload mechanisms
  silently accept corruption under loss", the single red accent used ONLY here
- a "Findings by mechanism" section as a clean LIST: each row has a left status
  label (VULNERABLE in red / VERIFIED in green) in a fixed-width column, then the
  finding in dark ink with muted secondary detail lines; rows separated by
  HAIRLINE RULES — no boxes, no cards
- a quiet "Why this matters" callout with a left rule, referencing the PicSat
  satellite loss
- a "Recommendation" paragraph
- a small monospace METHOD footer

Aesthetic: a white sheet on a light-gray background, near-black ink (#16191c),
ONE serious accent red (#b3261e) used for risk only, generous whitespace, line
length about 75 characters, A4 / print-friendly in grayscale. Typography: a
refined serif (Georgia / Tiempos feel) for the headline, a clean grotesk sans for
body, and monospace for labels, the document id, file paths and hashes.

HARD CONSTRAINTS — do NOT include: gradients, glossy or rounded "cards", drop
shadows, emoji, icons, stock space/rocket photography, bright multi-color
palettes, badges, pills with backgrounds, or any SaaS-landing-page styling.
Prefer subtraction over addition.
